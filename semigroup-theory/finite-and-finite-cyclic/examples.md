The common categorical object behind all these examples is:

$$
\boxed{\text{an endomorphism }f:X\to X\text{ and its iterates}}
$$

The different fields arise because (X) lives in different categories and the category gives different notions of "eventual behavior".

The universal construction is:

$$
\mathbb{N}\rightarrow \operatorname{End}_{\mathcal C}(X)
$$

where

$$
n\mapsto f^n
$$

is the monoid action of ((\mathbb N,+)) generated by (f).

Equivalently, (X) is an algebra over the free monoid:

$$
\mathbb N\curvearrowright X.
$$

Everything below is a specialization.

---

# 1. Finite semigroup theory

## Category

$$
\mathbf{FinSet}
$$

Objects:

$$
X={x_1,\dots,x_n}
$$

Morphisms:

$$
f:X\to X
$$

are functions.

---

## Endomorphism

Choose:

$$
a\in S
$$

and define

$$
L_a:S\to S
$$

by

$$
L_a(x)=ax.
$$

Then:

$$
L_a^n(x)=a^nx.
$$

The cyclic semigroup is:

$$
\langle a\rangle
================

{a,a^2,a^3,\dots}.
$$

---

## Structure

Finite object:

$$
|\langle a\rangle|<\infty
$$

forces:

$$
a^m=a^{m+p}.
$$

Therefore:

$$
\langle a\rangle
================

\text{transient}
\cup
\text{kernel}.
$$

The kernel is the minimal invariant subobject.

---

# 2. Floyd cycle detection

## Category

Still:

$$
\mathbf{FinSet}
$$

but without multiplication.

Object:

$$
X
$$

Morphisms:

$$
f:X\to X.
$$

---

The orbit functor:

$$
\mathbb N\to X
$$

is:

$$
n\mapsto f^n(x).
$$

The orbit decomposition:

$$
{x,f(x),f^2(x),...}
$$

becomes:

$$
\text{tail}\rightarrow\text{cycle}.
$$

The invariants are:

$$
\mu=\text{tail length}
$$

and

$$
\lambda=\text{cycle length}.
$$

Correspondence:

$$
\boxed{
\mu=\text{index},\qquad
\lambda=\text{period}
}
$$

---

# 3. Deterministic finite automata

## Category

$$
\mathbf{Set}
$$

with an alphabet action.

A DFA is a functor:

$$
F:\Sigma^*\rightarrow\mathbf{Set}
$$

where

$$
\Sigma^*
$$

is the free monoid of words.

A word

$$
w\in\Sigma^*
$$

acts:

$$
\delta_w:X\rightarrow X.
$$

---

The transition is:

$$
x\mapsto\delta(x,a)
$$

and iteration gives:

$$
x
\mapsto
\delta(x,a)
\mapsto
\delta(x,a^2)
$$

etc.

The recurrent SCCs are the automata version of the semigroup kernel.

---

# 4. Markov chains

Now the category changes.

## Category

Instead of:

$$
\mathbf{Set}
$$

we move to:

$$
\mathbf{Vect}
$$

or more precisely stochastic spaces.

A Markov chain is:

$$
P:X\rightarrow \Delta(X)
$$

where

$$
\Delta(X)
$$

is the probability simplex.

So a state maps to a distribution.

---

Matrix representation:

$$
P:X\to X
$$

becomes

$$
P\in M_n(\mathbb R)
$$

with:

$$
P^n
$$

representing (n)-step evolution.

---

Decomposition:

$$
X=
T\sqcup R
$$

where:

* (T): transient states
* (R): recurrent states

Matrix:

$$
P=
\begin{bmatrix}
Q&0\\
R&S
\end{bmatrix}
$$

where:

$$
Q^n\rightarrow0
$$

and

$$
S^n
$$

stays in the recurrent part.

---

# 5. Ergodic theory

Here we move to measurable spaces.

## Category

$$
\mathbf{Meas}
$$

or

$$
\mathbf{Prob}
$$

Objects:

$$
(X,\Sigma,\mu)
$$

Morphisms:

measure-preserving maps:

$$
T:X\rightarrow X
$$

such that:

$$
\mu(T^{-1}(A))=\mu(A).
$$

---

The action:

$$
\mathbb N\curvearrowright X
$$

is:

$$
n,x\mapsto T^n(x).
$$

The invariant object is:

$$
A=T^{-1}(A).
$$

---

Ergodicity means:

The only invariant subobjects are trivial:

$$
\mu(A)=0
$$

or

$$
\mu(A)=1.
$$

Categorically:

the fixed-point subobject is terminal/trivial.

---

# 6. Poincaré–Bendixson

Now:

## Category

$$
\mathbf{Top}
$$

Objects:

topological spaces.

Morphisms:

continuous maps.

---

A flow is:

$$
\phi:\mathbb R\times X\rightarrow X
$$

satisfying:

$$
\phi_{t+s}
==========

\phi_t\circ\phi_s.
$$

So:

$$
\mathbb R\rightarrow End(X).
$$

---

Orbit:

$$
{ \phi_t(x):t\in\mathbb R}
$$

The limit object is:

$$
\omega(x)
=========

\bigcap_{t>0}
\overline{
{\phi_s(x):s>t}
}.
$$

Poincaré–Bendixson classifies possible (\omega)-limit objects in dimension 2.

---

# 7. Linear algebra / matrices

## Category

$$
\mathbf{Vect}_k
$$

Object:

vector space (V).

Morphisms:

linear maps.

---

Operator:

$$
T:V\rightarrow V.
$$

Iteration:

$$
T,T^2,T^3,\ldots
$$

---

The analogue of transient/kernel:

Generalized eigenspace decomposition:

$$
V=
V_{\lambda=0}
\oplus
V_{\lambda\neq0}.
$$

The nilpotent part:

$$
N^k=0
$$

is the transient component.

The invertible part:

$$
T|*{V*{\lambda\neq0}}
$$

is the recurrent component.

This is the linear algebra analogue of:

$$
\text{tail}\rightarrow\text{cycle}.
$$

---

# 8. Universal table

| Field             | Category                           | Endomorphism           | Kernel analogue         |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Semigroup         | (\mathbf{FinSet}) + multiplication | (L_a)                  | minimal ideal           |
| Floyd             | (\mathbf{FinSet})                  | (f:X\to X)             | cycle                   |
| Automata          | free monoid action                 | transition             | recurrent SCC           |
| Markov            | stochastic spaces                  | transition matrix      | recurrent class         |
| Ergodic theory    | probability spaces                 | measure-preserving (T) | invariant sigma algebra |
| Dynamical systems | (\mathbf{Top})                     | flow (\phi_t)          | (\omega)-limit set      |
| Linear algebra    | (\mathbf{Vect})                    | operator (T)           | invariant subspace      |

---

The deepest unification is:

$$
\boxed{
\text{A system is an object }X\text{ with an action by a monoid }M.
}
$$

Then the question becomes:

> What does repeated action of (M) do to (X)?

Semigroup theory studies this algebraically, Floyd studies one orbit algorithmically, Markov theory studies it probabilistically, and dynamical systems study it geometrically. They are all different representations of **monoid actions and their invariant subobjects**.
